What is PIPEDA?

The Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act (PIPEDA) is Canada's primary federal law governing how private-sector organizations collect, use, and disclose personal information.

It is based on 10 fair information principles, to which ParcoursCA fully adheres.

Our 10 PIPEDA Commitments

1. Accountability

Fjord Tech Finance (ParcoursCA) is responsible for personal information under its care. A Privacy Officer has been designated and can be reached at: support@parcoursca.ca

2. Identifying Purposes

We identify the purposes for which information is collected before or at the time of collection. Each piece of information collected corresponds to a specific service you have chosen to use.

3. Consent

We obtain your informed consent before collecting, using, or disclosing your personal information. You may withdraw your consent at any time.

4. Limiting Collection

We collect only the information necessary for the identified purposes — no more, no less. We do not engage in preventive data collection.

5. Limiting Use, Disclosure, and Retention

Your data is used only for the purposes for which it was collected. We apply a minimum retention policy and delete data that is no longer needed.

6. Accuracy

We keep your information up to date and accurate. You may correct your information at any time from your profile or by contacting us.

7. Safeguards

AES-256-GCM encryption of sensitive data, TLS 1.3 for transmissions, available multi-factor authentication, and regular security audits. Your database is hosted exclusively in Canada (AWS ca-central-1 via Supabase). Peripheral services (payments, notifications, AI) transit through SOC 2-certified US servers, without permanent storage of your personal data.

8. Openness

Our personal information management policies are publicly available. We inform users of any changes to our privacy practices.

9. Individual Access

Upon request, we will inform you of the existence, use, and disclosure of your personal information, and provide you access to that information within 30 days.

10. Challenging Compliance

You may challenge our compliance with these principles. If your concern is not resolved, you may file a complaint with the Office of the Privacy Commissioner of Canada (www.priv.gc.ca).

Law 25 — Quebec

In addition to PIPEDA, ParcoursCA complies with the requirements of Quebec's Act to Modernize Legislative Provisions Respecting the Protection of Personal Information (Law 25), including:

  • Privacy Impact Assessments (PIA) for new projects
  • Mandatory notification in the event of a privacy incident
  • Right to data portability
  • Right to de-indexation and to be forgotten
  • Specific consent for the use of data for profiling purposes

Report an Incident

In the event of a security incident or data breach, we commit to notifying you within 72 hours, in accordance with our legal obligations, and to taking appropriate corrective measures immediately.

To report an incident or vulnerability: support@parcoursca.ca
